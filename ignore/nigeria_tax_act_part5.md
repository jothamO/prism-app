# Nigeria Tax Act 2025 - Part 5: Stamp Duties

## CHAPTER FIVE – TAXATION OF DUTIABLE INSTRUMENTS

### Section 124 - Charge of Duties
Duties imposed on instruments at rates in Eighth Schedule if:
- (a) First executed in Nigeria, OR
- (b) Executed outside Nigeria but relates to Nigerian property/matters

---

## DUTY DENOTATION (Section 125)

**Duty may be paid by:**
1. Tax stamps
2. Die
3. Electronic/digital tagging
4. Electronic receipt
5. Certificate issuance
6. Other means determined by tax authority

---

## OBLIGATION TO STAMP (Section 126)

**Timing:** Within 30 days of execution

**Responsible Party:**
- Transferee of real property
- Beneficiary of service
- Person taking security in transaction

---

## ADMISSIBILITY (Section 127)

**General Rule:**
Unstamped instruments NOT admissible in:
- Court proceedings
- Judicial proceedings
- Arbitration proceedings
- Evidentiary requirements

**Exception:**
Unstamped instruments admissible in criminal proceedings

---

## CHARGEABLE INSTRUMENTS

### Section 128 - Bill of Exchange
Includes: drafts, orders, cheques, letters of credit

**Excludes:** Bank notes

### Section 129 - Promissory Note
Any document promising payment of money

**Excludes:** Bank notes

### Section 130 - Options
Contracts for options to purchase/sell stocks/securities

### Section 131 - Conveyance on Sale
**All transfers of interest/rights in real property**

### Section 132 - Conveyance for Debt
Debt is deemed the consideration for ad valorem duty

### Section 133 - Transfer of Mineral Assets
Agreements for transfer of mineral assets or interests

### Section 134 - Exchange of Property
Consideration exceeding N1,000,000 or annual minimum wage (whichever higher)

### Section 135 - Leases
Agreements for land/building leases

**Exemption:**
Annual value < N10,000,000 or 10x minimum wage (whichever higher)

---

## COMPANY INSTRUMENTS

### Section 136 - Share Capital Duty
Ad valorem duty on share capital or increase in share capital
(See Ninth Schedule for rates)

### Section 137 - Loan Capital Duty
Ad valorem duty on:
- Debenture stock
- Other stock or funded debt
- Any debt raised by corporation/company

**Excludes:**
- Overdrafts
- Loans ≤ 12 months
- On-lending arrangements (duty on final beneficiary)

### Section 138 - Marketable Securities
Instruments for issuing marketable securities by Nigerian entities

### Section 139 - Appraisements
Duty on valuations of real property by appraisers

---

## MISCELLANEOUS PROVISIONS

### Section 140 - Duplicates & Counterparts
Must be stamped as original OR certified that full duty paid on original

### Section 141 - Multiple Transactions
Each transaction in single instrument charged separately

### Section 142 - Multiple Instruments for Same Transaction
- ONE instrument charged with ad valorem duty
- Others stamped as counterparts at flat rates (Ninth Schedule)

### Section 143 - Non-Monetary Consideration
Value = Market value of consideration

---

## EXEMPTIONS (Section 185 - Part III, Chapter Eight)

**Exempt from Stamp Duties:**
1. Government or legislative stocks/funds
2. Ships or vessels (sale/transfer)
3. Instruments where Government is payer
4. Consular officer instruments (with reciprocity)
5. Co-operative society instruments
6. Shares/securities in regulated securities lending transactions
7. All documents for stock/share transfers
8. Electronic transfers/receipts < N10,000
9. Salary payments and intra-bank self-transfers

**Special Exemption - Mergers:**
No stamp duties on asset segregation for petroleum mining leases converting from old to new regime

---

## KEY RATES (Eighth Schedule)

### Conveyance on Sale (Real Property):
Generally ad valorem based on consideration value

### Share Capital:
Per Ninth Schedule (ad valorem on amount of capital)

### Loan Capital:
Per Ninth Schedule (ad valorem on loan amount)

### Leases:
Ad valorem based on lease term and annual value

### Appraisements:
Charged to appraiser based on valuation amount

---

## COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

1. **Stamp within 30 days** of execution
2. **Keep stamped copies** of all dutiable instruments
3. **Unstamped instruments invalid** for legal proceedings
4. **Electronic stamping** may be mandated by regulations
5. **Penalties** for non-compliance per Tax Administration Act

---

## PRACTICAL EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Property Sale
- Sale of land: N10,000,000
- Stamp duty: Ad valorem rate applied
- **Buyer responsible** for stamping within 30 days
- Document inadmissible in court if unstamped

### Example 2: Lease Agreement
- Annual rent: N8,000,000
- Value below N10,000,000 threshold
- **No stamp duty required**

### Example 3: Share Capital Increase
- Company increases share capital by N50,000,000
- Stamp duty: Ad valorem per Ninth Schedule
- Duty paid on capital increase amount

### Example 4: Loan Capital
- Company raises N100,000,000 debenture
- Stamp duty: Ad valorem per Ninth Schedule
- **Excludes:** Short-term loans and overdrafts
