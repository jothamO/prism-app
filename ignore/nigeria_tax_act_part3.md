# Nigeria Tax Act 2025 - Part 3: Companies Income Tax

## TAXATION OF COMPANIES (Chapter 2, Parts II & VI)

### Section 6 - Nigerian Company
**Scope:** Profits of a Nigerian company are deemed to accrue in Nigeria wherever they arise.

**Controlled Foreign Company (CFC) Rules:**
- Undistributed profits of foreign subsidiary attributable to Nigerian parent must be included
- Top-up tax where foreign subsidiary pays below minimum effective tax rate

---

## PART VI – ASCERTAINMENT OF TOTAL PROFITS

### Section 27 - Total Profits of Companies

**Formula:**  
Total Profits = Assessable Profits - Losses - Capital Allowances

**Components:**
1. **Assessable Profits:** From all sources including chargeable gains
2. **Less Losses:** From previous years (subject to limitations)
3. **Less Capital Allowances:** Per First Schedule rates

**Key Rules:**
- Loss deductions cannot exceed the loss amount
- Losses only deductible from the trade/business where incurred
- Losses carried forward until fully recouped
- Digital asset losses only deductible against digital asset profits

---

## PART IX – RATES OF TAX

### Section 56 - Rate of Tax for Companies

| Company Type | Rate |
|--------------|------|
| Small Company | 0% |
| All Other Companies | 30% |

**Small Company Definition:**
- Gross turnover ≤ N50,000,000 per annum, AND
- Total fixed assets ≤ N250,000,000
- Professional services firms excluded

---

### Section 57 - Effective Tax Rate (Minimum Tax)

**15% Minimum Effective Tax Rate applies to:**
1. Constituent entities of MNE groups
2. Companies with turnover ≥ N20 billion

**Effective Tax Rate Calculation:**
- Aggregate covered tax paid ÷ Profits
- **Profits** = Net profits before tax less 5% of (depreciation + personnel costs)

**Top-up Tax Required if ETR < 15%**

---

## PART X – DEVELOPMENT LEVY

### Section 59 - Development Levy

**Rate:** 4% of assessable profits

**Applies to:**
- All companies chargeable under Chapters 2 and 3
- **Excludes:** Small companies and non-resident companies

**Distribution of Levy:**
- Tertiary Education Trust Fund: 50%
- Nigerian Education Loan: 15%
- National Information Technology Development Fund: 8%
- National Agency for Science and Engineering Infrastructure: 8%
- National Board for Technological Incubation: 4%
- Defence and Security Infrastructure Fund: 10%
- National Cybersecurity Fund: 5%

## DEDUCTIONS ALLOWED (Section 20)

**Fully Deductible:**
1. Interest on debt (subject to Third Schedule limits)
2. Rent and premiums for business premises
3. Salaries, wages and employee benefits
4. Repairs (not improvements)
5. Bad and doubtful debts (proven)
6. Approved pension contributions
7. Research and development expenses
8. Real estate investment company distributions
9. Disability-related expenses

**Interest Deductibility Limit (Third Schedule):**
- Interest to connected persons capped at 30% of EBITDA
- Excess interest disallowed
- Carry forward up to 5 years
- Exemption for banks and insurance companies

---

## DEDUCTIONS NOT ALLOWED (Section 21)

1. Capital expenditure or capital withdrawn
2. Domestic or private expenses
3. Taxes on income
4. Unapproved pension contributions
5. Depreciation (use capital allowances instead)
6. Amounts reserved from profits
7. Non-arm's length payments to connected persons
8. Expenses for exempt income
9. Penalties and fines
10. Taxes paid on behalf of others
11. **Expenses where VAT not charged or import duty not paid**

---

## CAPITAL ALLOWANCES (First Schedule, Part I)

### Rates by Asset Class:

| Class | Rate | Assets |
|-------|------|--------|
| **Class 1** | 10% | Buildings, Agricultural assets, Masts, Intangibles, Heavy transport |
| **Class 2** | 20% | Plant & equipment, Furniture, Mining equipment |
| **Class 3** | 25% | Motor vehicles, Software, Other capital expenditure |

**Key Rules:**
- Straight-line depreciation over asset life
- 1% retained for statistical purposes (doesn't reduce allowance)
- Asset must be in use at end of basis period
- Prorated for partial year
- VAT and import duty must be paid for expenditure to qualify

## SPECIAL PROVISIONS

### Section 9 - Dividend Substitution
Tax charged on dividends declared from untaxed profits

**Exemptions:**
- Dividends from retained earnings (already taxed)
- Dividends from exempt income
- Franked investment income
- Real estate investment company distributions (if 75%+ distributed)

### Section 10 - Undistributed Profits (Close Companies)
Applies to companies controlled by ≤5 individuals:
- Service may deem profits distributed
- Taxed in shareholders' hands proportionately
- Assessment valid within 3 years of receiving audited accounts

---

## NON-RESIDENT COMPANIES (Section 17)

**Taxable in Nigeria if:**
1. Permanent establishment or significant economic presence in Nigeria
2. Payment from Nigerian resident for services rendered outside Nigeria
3. Insurance premiums for Nigerian risks

**Permanent Establishment includes:**
- Fixed place of business
- Agent authorized to conduct business
- Stock of goods for delivery
- Projects (surveys, construction, installation)
- Services through employees/agents

**Significant Economic Presence:**
- Digital services transmitted to Nigeria
- Includes e-commerce, app stores, online advertising, cloud computing, etc.

**Profit Attribution Methods:**
1. Actual attribution per Section 17(5)
2. Profit margin method (Section 17(6))
3. **Minimum tax:** Higher of tax withheld OR 4% of income

---

## ANTI-AVOIDANCE (Section 192)

**Transfer Pricing:**
- Transactions with related parties must be at arm's length
- Service may adjust prices
- Detailed regulations apply

**Section 191 - Artificial Transactions:**
- Service may disregard artificial or fictitious dispositions
- Connected person transactions presumed artificial unless arm's length
