# Nigeria Tax Act 2025 - Part 8: Petroleum Operations Taxation

## CHAPTER THREE – TAXATION OF INCOME FROM PETROLEUM OPERATIONS

---

## OVERVIEW

**Three Tax Regimes:**

1. **Part I:** Hydrocarbon Tax (NEW - Petroleum Industry Act leases)
2. **Part II:** Petroleum Profits Tax (OLD - Pre-PIA leases not yet converted)
3. **Part III:** Deep Offshore & Inland Basin PSCs

**Plus:** All petroleum operations subject to **Chapter 2 Income Tax**

---

## PART I – HYDROCARBON TAX (Sections 65-89)

### Application (Section 65)

**Applies to:**
- Upstream petroleum operations
- Onshore, shallow water, deep offshore
- Licences/leases under Petroleum Industry Act

**Covers:**
- Crude oil
- Field condensates
- Liquid natural gas liquids from associated gas

**Excludes:**
- Associated natural gas (gaseous)
- Non-associated natural gas
- Condensates from non-associated gas
- Products downstream of measurement points

---

### Section 66 - Charge of Hydrocarbon Tax

**Levied on:** Profits from crude oil operations

**Basis:** Per accounting period (1 Jan - 31 Dec)

---

### Section 67 - Ascertainment of Profits

**Crude Oil Revenue:**
- Proceeds of all chargeable oil sold
- Value of all chargeable oil disposed

**Adjusted Profit:**
- Revenue less allowable deductions (Section 68)

**Assessable Profit:**
- Adjusted profit less losses (Section 70)

**Chargeable Profit:**
- Assessable profit less allowances (Section 71)

---

### Section 68 - Allowable Deductions

**Deductible:**
1. Rents for petroleum mining lease/prospecting licence
2. **Royalties** (crude oil, associated gas, and PSC payments)
3. Repair expenses (plant, machinery, fixtures)
4. **First exploration well + first 2 appraisal wells** (tangible/intangible)
5. **Decommissioning & abandonment fund** (approved, cash-backed)
6. Levies, stamp duties, fees to Government
7. Gas reinjection wells (ratified by Commission)
8. **Host community development trusts** (approved contributions)

**Note:** Subsequent exploration/appraisal wells = qualifying drilling expenditure (capital allowances)

---

### Section 69 - Deductions NOT Allowed

**Non-Deductible:**
1. Purchase of petroleum deposit information
2. Penalties, gas flare fees
3. Financial/bank charges, arbitration, litigation, bad debts, interest
4. Head office/affiliate costs, shared costs, R&D
5. Production/signature bonuses, licence renewal fees
6. Tax paid on behalf of vendor/contractor (net-of-tax contracts)
7. Capital withdrawn
8. Capital improvements
9. Insurance recoveries
10. Non-business premises rent/repairs
11. Income taxes, development levy, other taxes
12. Depreciation
13. Unapproved pension contributions
14. Customs duties
15. **Expenses where VAT not charged or import duty not paid**
16. Costs per Sixth Schedule para 2(2)(c)

---

### Section 70 - Assessable Profits

**Calculation:**
Adjusted profit - Losses

**Loss Carry Forward:**
- To extent possible in first succeeding year
- Then subsequent years until fully recouped
- Separate for each class (Section 72 categories)

**Election:**
- May defer loss deduction (within 5 months or extended time)

---

### Section 71 - Chargeable Profits & Allowances

**Deductions from Assessable Profit:**

**(a) Capital Allowances** (Part II, First Schedule):
- 20% per annum for 5 years
- Qualifying plant, pipeline, building, drilling expenditure

**(b) Production Allowances** (Sixth Schedule):
- Lower of US$2.50/barrel OR 20% of fiscal oil price (converted leases)
- New leases: Lower of US$8/barrel OR 20% fiscal price (first 50M barrels onshore, 100M barrels shallow water), then US$4/barrel

**(c) Acquisition Costs of Petroleum Rights:**
- Rights: 20% per annum (annual allowance)
- Assets: Per applicable depreciation rates (First Schedule Part II)

**Cost-Price Ratio Limit (Sixth Schedule):**
- Total costs capped at **65% of gross revenues** at measurement points
- Excess costs carried forward (subject to 65% cap in future years)
- Unrecouped costs at termination: not deductible

---

### Section 72 - Chargeable Hydrocarbon Tax Rates

**TWO CLASSES:**

**(a) 30% Rate:**
- Petroleum mining leases per PIA Section 93(6)(b) & 93(7)(b)
- Onshore and shallow water

**(b) 15% Rate:**
- Onshore and shallow water
- Petroleum prospecting licences per PIA Section 93(6)(a) & 93(7)(a)

---

### Section 73 - Additional Chargeable Tax

**Applies when:**
- Sale/disposal between connected persons, OR
- Disposal to any person

**If:**
Actual hydrocarbon tax < Tax based on **fiscal oil price**

**Additional Tax:**
Difference between fiscal price tax and actual tax

**Fiscal Oil Price:**
Established by Commission (export parity basis per PIA 7th Schedule)

**Payment:**
With final instalment of hydrocarbon tax

---

### Section 74 - Pre-Production Cost

**Treatment:**
- All costs before production/sale commencement
- Deemed qualifying pre-production capital expenditure
- **Amortised 20% per annum** on commencement (Part II, First Schedule)

---

### Section 76 - Consolidation

**Costs & Revenues:**
- **Income tax (Chapter 2):** Consolidate across all terrains
- **Hydrocarbon tax:** Consolidate only within same category (Section 72(a) or (b))

---

### Section 78 - Income Tax on Petroleum Operations

**Applies to:**
All petroleum operations (upstream, midstream, downstream) under PIA

**Income Tax Includes:**
- All incidental income from petroleum operations
- Chargeable gains on asset disposal

**Key Rules:**
- **Hydrocarbon tax NOT deductible** for income tax
- Royalties deductible per Section 82
- Decommissioning/abandonment funds deductible per Section 82
- Host community development trust contributions deductible

**Value Determination:**
Same as hydrocarbon tax (Section 78(2))

---

### Section 79 - Separate Companies Required

**Rule:**
Separate company for each stream:
- Upstream
- Midstream  
- Downstream

**Exception - Integrated Strategic Projects (ISP):**
- Oil & gas produced for processing to finished products
- Supplied wholesale solely to domestic market
- Can consolidate upstream + midstream for tax
- Arm's length transfer prices required

**Segregation Incentive:**
No stamp duties, VAT, or capital gains tax on segregation for converted leases

---

### Section 80 - Gas Incentives

**(1) Gas Pipeline Investment:**
- 5-year tax-free period AFTER economic development incentive expires

**(2) Natural Gas Transfers:**
- Upstream to midstream/downstream: subject to Chapter 2 income tax

**(3) Natural Gas Liquids & LPG:**
- From natural gas: subject to Chapter 2 income tax

---

### Section 81 - Allowances for Income Tax

**Acquisition Costs:**
- Petroleum rights: 20% per annum

**Other Assets:**
- Upstream: Part II, First Schedule (20% for most)
- Midstream/Downstream: Part I, First Schedule (10%, 20%, or 25%)

---

### Section 85 - Non-Associated Gas Greenfield Incentives

**Eligibility:**
- Non-associated gas greenfield developments
- Onshore and shallow water
- First commercial gas production between Act commencement and 1 Jan 2029

**Incentives Based on Hydrocarbon Liquids Ratio:**

| Liquids (barrels per MMscf) | Incentive | Rate/Limit |
|------------------------------|-----------|------------|
| ≤30 | Gas production tax credit | US$1.00/Mcf OR 30% fiscal gas price (lower) |
| 30-100 | Gas production tax credit | US$0.50/Mcf OR 30% fiscal gas price (lower) |
| >100 | No incentive | N/A |

**Duration:**
- Tax credit: 10 years from first gas production
- Then converts to production allowance (same rates)

**Recoupment:**
- Max tax credit per year: Tax payable on field for that year
- Unrecouped credit: Carry forward max 3 years

**Post-2029 Projects:**
- Production allowance only: US$0.50/Mcf OR 30% fiscal gas price (lower)
- If hydrocarbon liquids ≤100 barrels/MMscf

---

### Section 86 - Decommissioning & Abandonment Funds

**Deductibility Conditions:**
- Minimum 30% deposited with Nigerian bank (escrow)
- Bank accredited by CBN + Service criteria

---

### Section 89 - Petroleum Royalty

**Rates:** Seventh Schedule

**Administration:** Service (not Commission)

**Liability:** All production including tests

**Commission Accountability:**
- Must account for all royalties in 10-year period pre-Act
- Service has verification authority

---

## PART II – PETROLEUM PROFITS TAX (Sections 90-101)

### Application (Section 90)

**Applies to:**
- Oil prospecting licences (OPLs)
- Oil mining leases (OMLs)
- **NOT yet converted** under Petroleum Industry Act

**Tax:** Petroleum profits tax on profits per accounting period

---

### Section 91 - Revenue & Profits

**Revenue Includes:**
- Proceeds of chargeable oil sold
- Value of chargeable oil disposed
- Incidental income
- Chargeable gains (taxed at Section 56 rate: 30%)

**Adjusted Profit:**
Revenue - Deductions (Section 92) + Adjustments

**Assessable Profit:**
Adjusted profit - Losses (Section 97)

**Chargeable Profit:**
Assessable profit - Capital allowances (Section 98)

---

### Section 92 - Deductions Allowed

**Similar to hydrocarbon tax plus:**
- Non-productive rents
- Customs/excise duties (excluding certain goods)
- Intangible drilling costs (development wells)
- First exploration well + next 2 appraisal wells (same field)
- Approved pension contributions
- Customs, stamp duties, fees to Government
- Development levy (Section 59)
- Proven bad debts

---

### Section 95 - Deductions NOT Allowed

**Similar to hydrocarbon tax plus:**
- Information purchase re petroleum deposits
- Customs duty on resale goods or personal consumption
- Customs duty where Nigerian equivalent available cheaper
- Qualifying drilling expenditure
- Any expense/deduction already claimed elsewhere

---

### Section 98 - Chargeable Profits & Capital Allowances

**Capital Allowances:** Part III, First Schedule

**Limitation (Ringfence):**
To ensure tax ≥ 15% of assessable profit before allowances

**Allowance Calculation:**
Lower of:
- (a) Total capital allowances computed, OR
- (b) 85% of assessable profit LESS 170% of petroleum investment allowance

**Unutilized Allowances:**
Carried forward to next period

---

### Section 99 - Assessable Tax Rate

**Standard Rate:** 85% of chargeable profits

**Reduced Rate (Pre-Production):**
- 65.75% of chargeable profits
- Applies before full amortisation of pre-production costs
- Maximum 5 years from first accounting period
- **NOT available** to companies acquiring already-producing assets

---

### Section 100 - Additional Chargeable Tax

**Same as Section 73 for hydrocarbon tax**

Based on fiscal oil price vs actual consideration

---

## PART III – DEEP OFFSHORE & INLAND BASIN PSCs (Sections 102-117)

### Application (Section 102)

**Applies to:**
- Production sharing contracts
- Deep offshore and inland basin
- **Not yet converted** under PIA OR renegotiated per PIA

---

### Section 104 - PPT Rate

**50% of chargeable profits** for PSC areas

**Plus:** All other taxes (not exempted)

---

### Section 105 - Investment Tax Allowance

**50% of qualifying capital expenditure**

Granted when asset first used

---

### Section 106 - Royalty (Seventh Schedule)

Per PIA Seventh Schedule provisions

---

### Sections 108-111 - Oil Allocations (Monthly)

**Order of Allocation:**

1. **Royalty Oil:** To Commission/holder (for royalty + concession rental payment)

2. **Cost Oil:** To contractor (for operating cost recovery in US$)

3. **Tax Oil:** To Commission/holder (for PPT payment)

4. **Profit Oil:** Balance split per PSC terms

---

### Section 112 - Payment Responsibility

**Holder pays:**
- All royalty
- All concession rentals
- All PPT (on behalf of self + contractor)

**Service issues:**
Separate tax receipts for holder and contractor portions

---

### Section 113 - Tax Split

**Chargeable tax split:**
Between holder and contractor in same ratio as profit oil split

---

### Section 114 - Realisable Price

**For royalty and PPT calculation:**
Per PSC definition and holder's determination

---

### Section 117 - PSC Review

**Minister (Petroleum) must cause Commission:**
Review PSCs every 8 years

---

## PART IV – MISCELLANEOUS (Sections 118-119)

### Section 118 - Royalty Administration

**Service administers** all petroleum royalties per Tax Administration Act

**Commission must account** for all royalties (10-year pre-Act period)

---

### Section 119 - Definitions

**Key Terms:**

**"Accounting Period":**
- 1 January to 31 December
- OR shorter period from first sale to 31 Dec
- OR 1 Jan to cessation date

**"Chargeable Oil":**
Crude oil, condensate, natural gas liquids upstream of measurement point

**"Measurement Point":**
Determined in field development plan (or deemed point)

**"Associated Gas":**
- Gas-cap gas overlying crude oil
- Solution gas dissolved in crude oil

**"Non-Associated Gas":**
Gas not containing significant crude oil

**"Commission":**
Nigerian Upstream Petroleum Regulatory Commission

**"Authority":**
Nigerian Midstream & Downstream Petroleum Regulatory Authority

**"Fiscal Oil Price":**
Per Commission (PIA 7th Schedule)

**"Fiscal Gas Price":**
Per Commission (PIA 7th Schedule)

## ROYALTY RATES (Seventh Schedule)

### Hydrocarbon Tax Regime (Part I):

**Production-Based:**

| Terrain | Rate |
|---------|------|
| Onshore | 15% |
| Shallow water (up to 200m) | 12.5% |
| Deep offshore (beyond 200m) | 7.5% |
| Frontier basins | 7.5% |

**Production-Based (Small Fields):**
- Onshore/shallow ≤10,000 bopd: 5% (first 5,000), 7.5% (next 5,000)
- Deep offshore ≤50,000 bopd: 5% (up to 50,000)

**Price-Based (Additional):**
- Below $50/barrel: 0%
- At $100/barrel: 5%
- At $150+/barrel: 10%
- Linear interpolation between
- Price levels increase 2% annually from 2020 base
- **For Nigerian Sovereign Investment Authority**

**Natural Gas:**
- 5% of chargeable volume
- In-country utilisation: 2.5%

---

### PPT Regime (Part II):

**Crude Oil & Casinghead Spirit:**

| Terrain | Rate |
|---------|------|
| Onshore | 20% |
| Offshore up to 100m | 18.5% |
| Offshore 100-200m | 16.5% |
| Offshore beyond 200m | 10% |
| Frontier basin | 7.5% |
| Inland basin | 7.5% |

**Natural Gas:**
- Onshore: 7%
- Offshore: 5%
- Or gas flare penalty rate (whichever higher)

---

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. **Three separate petroleum tax regimes** (transitional)
2. **Hydrocarbon tax:** New PIA leases only
3. **PPT:** Old non-converted leases
4. **All petroleum operations:** Also subject to Chapter 2 income tax
5. **Royalties:** Separate from profit taxes
6. **Capital allowances:** Generally 20% across board
7. **Cost recovery capped:** 65% for hydrocarbon tax
8. **Gas incentives:** Significant for non-associated gas
9. **Fiscal prices:** Prevent transfer pricing manipulation
10. **Consolidation limited:** Within same tax category only
