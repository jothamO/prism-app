# Nigeria Tax Act 2025 - Part 1: Overview & Objectives

**Act No. 7 of 2025**  
**Commencement: 1st January, 2026**

## CHAPTER ONE â€“ OBJECTIVE AND APPLICATION

### Section 1 - Objective
The objective of this Act is to provide a unified fiscal legislation governing taxation in Nigeria.

### Section 2 - Application
This Act applies throughout Nigeria to any person required to comply with any provision of the tax laws whether personally or on behalf of another person.

---

## Key Definitions (Section 202 - Selected)

- **"Nigeria"** means the Federal Republic of Nigeria, and when used in a geographical sense, includes the territorial waters of the Federal Republic of Nigeria, and any area outside the territorial waters, including the continental shelf
- **"Person"** includes a company, partnership, community, family, individual, executor, trustee and legal arrangement
- **"Tax"** means any imposition, duty, levy, royalty or revenue accruing to government in full or in part under this Act or any other law
- **"Year of assessment"** means government tax year being 1 January to 31 December of any year
- **"Resident company"** means a Nigerian company
- **"Small company"** means a company that earns gross turnover of N50,000,000 or less per annum with total fixed assets not exceeding N250,000,000

From the commencement of this Act, the following enactments are repealed:
1. Capital Gains Tax Act, Cap. C1, LFN 2004
2. Casino Act, Cap. C3, LFN 2004
3. Companies Income Tax Act, Cap. C21, LFN 2004
4. Deep Offshore and Inland Basin Act, Cap. D3, LFN 2004
5. Industrial Development (Income Tax Relief) Act, Cap. I17, LFN 2004

7. Petroleum Profits Tax Act, Cap. P13, LFN 2004
8. Stamp Duties Act, Cap. S8, LFN 2004
9. Value Added Tax Act, Cap. V1, LFN 2004

## Structure of the Act

The Nigeria Tax Act 2025 consolidates all major tax legislation into nine chapters:

**Chapter 1:** Objective and Application  
**Chapter 2:** Taxation of Income of Persons  
**Chapter 3:** Taxation of Income from Petroleum Operations  
**Chapter 4:** Relief for Double Taxation  
**Chapter 5:** Taxation of Dutiable Instruments  
**Chapter 6:** Value Added Tax  
**Chapter 7:** Surcharge  
**Chapter 8:** Tax Incentives  
**Chapter 9:** General Provisions
