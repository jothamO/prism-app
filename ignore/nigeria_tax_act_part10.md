# Nigeria Tax Act 2025 - Part 10: Key Schedules Summary

## THE FOURTEEN SCHEDULES

---

## FIRST SCHEDULE - CAPITAL ALLOWANCES

### Three Parts by Tax Regime:

**Part I:** Chapter 2 Income Tax (Companies & Individuals)  
**Part II:** Hydrocarbon Tax (Petroleum Industry Act)  
**Part III:** Petroleum Profits Tax (Old OMLs/OPLs)

---

### PART I - CHAPTER 2 CAPITAL ALLOWANCES

**Three Classes - All Straight-Line:**

| Class | Rate | Assets |
|-------|------|--------|
| **1** | **10%** | Buildings, Agricultural assets, Masts, Intangible assets, Heavy transport equipment |
| **2** | **20%** | Plant & equipment, Furniture & fittings, Mining equipment, Agricultural equipment |
| **3** | **25%** | Motor vehicles, Software, Other capital expenditure |

**Key Rules:**
- Asset must be in use at end of basis period
- 1% retained for statistical purposes (doesn't reduce allowance)
- Hire purchase: allowance on instalments paid (exclude interest)
- Partnership: allowances apportioned per profit-sharing ratio
- Unutilized allowances carried forward indefinitely

**Qualifying Capital Expenditure:**
- VAT must be charged (if applicable)
- Import duty must be paid (if applicable)
- Otherwise expense not eligible

---

### PART II - HYDROCARBON TAX CAPITAL ALLOWANCES

**Uniform 20% Rate (5 years):**

| Asset Type | Rate | Years |
|------------|------|-------|
| Qualifying Plant | 20% | 5 |
| Qualifying Pipeline & Storage | 20% | 5 |
| Qualifying Building | 20% | 5 |
| Qualifying Drilling | 20% | 5 |

**Special Rules:**
- First exploration well + first 2 appraisal wells: 100% deductible (Section 68)
- Additional exploration/appraisal: capital allowance
- Pre-production costs: amortised 20% per annum
- 1% statistical retention rule applies

---

### PART III - PETROLEUM PROFITS TAX CAPITAL ALLOWANCES

**Same as Part II: 20% across board**

**Plus: Petroleum Investment Allowance (PIA):**

| Terrain | PIA Rate |
|---------|----------|
| Onshore | 5% |
| Territorial waters/continental shelf (up to 100m depth) | 10% |
| Territorial waters/continental shelf (100-200m depth) | 15% |

**PIA Notes:**
- Granted in year asset first used
- Added to capital allowance
- Subject to ringfence limitation (Section 98)

**Ringfence Rule:**
Allowances limited to ensure tax ≥ 15% of assessable profit

---

## SECOND SCHEDULE - EXPORT PROCESSING/FREE TRADE ZONES

### Tax Treatment (Section 60)

**Full Exemption (Subject to Section 57 - 15% minimum ETR):**
- 100% export sales OR inputs for export
- ≤25% sales to Nigerian customs territory

**Partial Taxation:**
- >25% customs sales: Tax on ALL customs sales

**Full Taxation from 1 January 2028:**
- All customs sales taxed (regardless of percentage)
- President may extend max 10 years from commencement

**Compliance Still Required:**
- Registration
- File returns
- Deduct tax at source
- Evidence of export proceeds

---

## THIRD SCHEDULE - INTEREST DEDUCTIBILITY

### Section 20(1)(a) & 92(1)(g)

**Rule:**
Interest to connected persons capped at **30% of EBITDA**

**EBITDA:**
Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, Amortisation

**Excess Interest:**
- Not deductible in current year
- Carry forward up to **5 years**
- Subject to 30% EBITDA cap in future years

**Exemptions:**
- Banking business
- Insurance business

**"Debt" Defined:**
- Loans
- Financial instruments
- Finance leases
- Financial derivatives
- Any arrangement giving rise to deductible interest/finance charges

---

## FOURTH SCHEDULE - INDIVIDUAL TAX RATES

### Section 58 - Progressive Tax Bands

| Taxable Income Band | Rate |
|---------------------|------|
| First N800,000 | 0% |
| Next N2,200,000 (N800,001 - N3,000,000) | 15% |
| Next N9,000,000 (N3,000,001 - N12,000,000) | 18% |
| Next N13,000,000 (N12,000,001 - N25,000,000) | 21% |
| Next N25,000,000 (N25,000,001 - N50,000,000) | 23% |
| Above N50,000,000 | 25% |

**Calculation Example:**

**Chargeable Income: N60,000,000**

- First N800,000 @ 0% = N0
- Next N2,200,000 @ 15% = N330,000
- Next N9,000,000 @ 18% = N1,620,000
- Next N13,000,000 @ 21% = N2,730,000
- Next N25,000,000 @ 23% = N5,750,000
- Remaining N10,000,000 @ 25% = N2,500,000

**Total Tax: N12,930,000** (21.55% effective rate)

## FIFTH SCHEDULE - SETTLEMENTS, TRUSTS & ESTATES

### Three Parts:

**Part I:** General rules for trust/estate income  
**Part II:** Special provisions for settlements on unmarried children  
**Part III:** Supplementary provisions

---

### Key Concepts:

**Computed Income:**
- Ascertained as if trust/estate is individual
- Deduct trustee expenses and fixed annuities
- Add/deduct trade business adjustments

**Apportionment:**
- Per specific proportions in deed/will
- Or per trustee discretion
- Or to trustee if remainder

**Attribution to Settlor (Part II):**
Income paid to unmarried infant child = settlor's income

**Exemption:** If aggregate to child ≤ National Minimum Wage

---

## SIXTH SCHEDULE - PRODUCTION ALLOWANCES & CPR

### Section 71(1)(b) & (2)

**Part 1: Production Allowances (Hydrocarbon Tax)**

**Converted OMLs:**
- Lower of US$2.50/barrel OR 20% fiscal oil price

**New Leases (Per Field):**

| Terrain | First Period | Rate | After Period | Rate |
|---------|--------------|------|--------------|------|
| Onshore | First 50M barrels | US$8 or 20% FOP* | After 50M | US$4 or 20% FOP* |
| Shallow Water | First 100M barrels | US$8 or 20% FOP* | After 100M | US$4 or 20% FOP* |

*FOP = Fiscal Oil Price (whichever lower)

---

**Part 2: Cost Price Ratio (CPR) Limit**

**Section 68 costs capped at 65% of gross revenues**

Excludes:
- Rents (Section 68(1)(a))
- Royalties (Section 68(1)(b))
- Host community trusts (Section 68(1)(h))

**Excess costs:**
- Carried forward to future years
- Subject to 65% cap in those years
- Unrecouped at termination: not deductible

---

## SEVENTH SCHEDULE - PETROLEUM ROYALTY

### Four Parts:

**Part I:** Administration  
**Part II:** Chargeable volumes & prices  
**Part III:** Hydrocarbon tax regime royalties  
**Part IV:** PPT & PSC regime royalties

---

### Part III - Hydrocarbon Tax Royalties

**Production-Based:**

| Terrain | Standard Rate |
|---------|---------------|
| Onshore | 15% |
| Shallow water (up to 200m) | 12.5% |
| Deep offshore (beyond 200m) | 7.5% |
| Frontier basins | 7.5% |

**Small Field Rates:**

**Onshore/Shallow (≤10,000 bopd):**
- First 5,000 bopd: 5%
- Next 5,000 bopd: 7.5%
- Over 10,000 bopd: standard rate

**Deep Offshore (≤50,000 bopd):**
- Up to 50,000 bopd: 5%
- Over 50,000 bopd: 7.5%

**Price-Based (Additional):**
- Below $50/barrel: 0%
- At $100/barrel: 5%
- At $150+/barrel: 10%
- Linear interpolation between
- Increases 2% annually from 2020 base
- **For Nigerian Sovereign Investment Authority**

**Natural Gas:**
- General: 5%
- In-country utilisation: 2.5%

---

### Part IV - PPT Royalties

**Crude Oil:**

| Terrain | Rate |
|---------|------|
| Onshore | 20% |
| Offshore up to 100m | 18.5% |
| Offshore 100-200m | 16.5% |
| Offshore beyond 200m | 10% |
| Frontier basin | 7.5% |
| Inland basin | 7.5% |

**Onshore/Shallow PSCs (Tranched):**

**Onshore Production:**
- <2,000 bopd: 5%
- 2,000-5,000 bopd: 7.5%
- 5,000-10,000 bopd: 15%
- >10,000 bopd: 20%

**Natural Gas:**
- Onshore: 7%
- Offshore: 5%
- Or gas flare penalty rate (whichever higher)

**Deep Offshore Price-Based:**
- $0-$20/barrel: 0%
- $20-$60/barrel: 2.5%
- $60-$100/barrel: 4%
- $100-$150/barrel: 8%
- >$150/barrel: 10%

## NINTH SCHEDULE - STAMP DUTIES RATES

### Referenced in Sections 136, 137, 142(2)

**Key Instruments:**

1. **Share Capital** (Section 136):
   - Ad valorem on amount of capital/increase

2. **Loan Capital** (Section 137):
   - Ad valorem on debentures, stock, funded debt
   - Excludes: overdrafts, loans ≤12 months, on-lending

3. **Counterparts** (Section 142(2)):
   - Flat rate on duplicate instruments for same transaction

4. **Conveyances**:
   - Ad valorem on consideration for real property transfers

5. **Leases**:
   - Ad valorem based on term and annual rent
   - Exempt if annual value <N10M or 10x minimum wage

*(Specific rates in Ninth Schedule of Act - not reproduced in full here)*

## ELEVENTH SCHEDULE - VAT SUSPENSION

### Section 186(2)

**Items on which VAT collection is SUSPENDED:**
1. Petroleum products (automotive gas oil, aviation fuel, premium motor spirit, household kerosene, locally produced LPG)
2. Renewable energy equipment
3. Compressed Natural Gas (CNG)
4. Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG)
5. Other gaseous hydrocarbons

**Minister may activate by Order** specifying commencement date

## TWELFTH SCHEDULE - DETERMINATION OF RESIDENCE

### Section 15(6) - Partnerships

**Complex rules for determining tax territory of residence**

**Key Principles:**

**Foreign Employment:**
- Resident where principal office of employer located

**Nigerian Employment:**
- Resident where place of residence on relevant date

**Partnership:**
- Active partner: where performs duties
- Dormant partner: where usually resides

**Pensions:**
- Where place of residence on 1 January
- Or where pension paid from (if no residence)

**Other Earned Income:**
- Where place of residence on 1 January
- Or where income derived from

**Unearned Income:**
- Where place of residence on 1 January
- Or where income arises

## THIRTEENTH SCHEDULE - AGRICULTURAL EXEMPTION

### Section 163(1)(p) & 187

**Five-Year Exemption for Agricultural Businesses**

**Eligible Activities:**

| Sub-Sector | Activity | Exempted Products |
|------------|----------|-------------------|
| **Crop Production** | Growing perennial/non-perennial crops | Raw/semi-processed crops |
| **Livestock** | Raising/breeding animals in ranches/farms | Live animals, raw products |
| **Livestock Processing** | Processing of livestock | Processed products |
| **Forestry** | Plantation of rubber, acacia trees | Raw forestry products |
| **Dairy** | Manufacture of dairy products | Dairy products |
| **Cocoa** | Processing of cocoa | Cocoa products |

**Exemption:**
- Income tax exempt for first 5 years
- From commencement of business
- Must be engaged in primary/processing activities
