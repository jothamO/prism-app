# Nigeria Tax Act 2025 - Part 6: Tax Incentives

## CHAPTER EIGHT – TAX INCENTIVES

---

## PART I – INCOME TAX EXEMPTIONS (Section 163)

### Fully Exempt Entities:
1. **Friendly societies** (non-trading profits)
2. **Co-operative societies** (non-trading profits)
3. **Educational, religious, charitable organizations** (public character, non-trading)
4. **Trade unions** (non-trading profits)
5. **Federal, State, Local Governments** (except trading activities)

### Exempt Investment Income:
- **Collective investment scheme dividends**
- **Real estate investment company dividends/rental income** (if 75%+ distributed within 12 months)
- **Regulated securities lending compensating payments** (dividends/interest)
- **Government bonds** (Federal/State)
- **Wholly export-oriented business dividends**

### Exempt Foreign Income:
- **Dividend, interest, rent, royalty** from outside Nigeria if brought through approved channels

### Exempt Personal Income:
- **National minimum wage earners** (employment income)
- **Military officers' wages and salaries**
- **Consular fees** (foreign State officials with reciprocity)
- **Diplomatic immunities** (per Diplomatic Immunities Act)

### Retirement & Compensation:
- **Pension funds and assets** (Pension Reform Act)
- **Pensions, gratuities, retirement benefits** (Pension Reform Act)
- **Wound and disability pensions** (armed forces)
- **Death gratuities**
- **Redundancy payments** (subject to Part VIII limits)
- **Compensation for personal injury:** First N50,000,000 exempt

### Startup & Investment:
- **Angel investor gains** from labelled startup disposal (if held 24+ months)
- **Venture capitalist gains** from labelled startup disposal (if held 24+ months)

### Agricultural Business (First 5 Years):
- **Crop production, livestock, aquaculture, forestry, dairy** (per Thirteenth Schedule)

### Sports:
- **Sporting activity companies**

### Export Promotion:
- **Profits from goods/services exported from Nigeria** (if proceeds repatriated through official channels)
- **Excludes:** Petroleum operations companies

### Combat Personnel:
- **Other ranks serving in combat zones, hazardous areas, designated operations**

---

## SECTION 163(3) – WAGE RELIEF (2023-2025)

**50% Additional Deduction** for costs incurred in 2023-2025 on:

### (a) Wage Awards/Increases for Low-Income Workers:
- Bringing gross monthly remuneration up to N100,000
- Awards above N100,000 monthly salary NOT eligible

### (b) Net New Employment:
- Salaries of new employees (net increase over 3-year average)
- Must not involuntarily disengage within 3 years

---

## SECTION 164 – DEDUCTIBLE DONATIONS

**Allowed Deductions:**
Donations (revenue or capital nature) to:
1. Public funds
2. Statutory bodies/institutions
3. Religious, charitable, educational, scientific institutions (Nigeria-based)
4. Diplomatic bodies
5. Pandemic/natural disaster/public emergency interventions

**Limit:** Maximum 10% of profit before tax

**Evidence Required**

---

## SECTION 165 – RESEARCH & DEVELOPMENT

**Deduction:** R&D expenditure incurred

**Limit:** Maximum 5% of turnover

**Tax on Proceeds:** If R&D outcome sold/transferred for exploitation

---

## PART II – ECONOMIC DEVELOPMENT TAX INCENTIVES (EDTI)

### Overview (Sections 166-184)
Replaces Industrial Development (Income Tax Relief) Act

**Purpose:** Incentivize priority sectors/industries

---

### Priority Sectors (Section 166 & Tenth Schedule)

**Sectors include:**
- Manufacturing
- Agriculture and agro-processing
- Information and communication technology
- Tourism and hospitality
- Creative industries
- Infrastructure development
- Healthcare services
- Education services
- Renewable energy
- Solid minerals processing

*(See Tenth Schedule for complete list with sunset dates)*

---

### Eligibility (Section 167)

**Qualifying Criteria:**
1. Nigerian-incorporated company (or exempted from incorporation)
2. Minimum capital investment per Tenth Schedule
3. Engaged in priority sector/product

---

### Application Process (Section 168)

**Submit to:** Nigerian Investment Promotion Commission (NIPC)

**Application Must Include:**
- Minimum capital commitment evidence
- Ultimate parent entity residence
- Asset particulars and costs
- Production day (actual/estimated)
- Non-priority products/by-products
- Loan and share capital details
- Ownership structure and directors
- Undertaking/declaration of truthfulness

**Fee:** 0.1% of qualifying capital expenditure (max N5,000,000)

---

### Approval (Section 169)

**Process:**
1. NIPC recommends to Minister (with tax expenditure report)
2. Minister recommends to President
3. **President approves** (certificate requires Presidential approval)

**Annual Reporting:** NIPC submits list to Minister → President & National Economic Council

---

### Certificate Terms (Section 170)

**Specifies:**
- Conditions
- Permissible by-products and proportions

**Key Rules:**
- No retroactive dates
- Production day must be certified by relevant authority
- Qualifying capital expenditure certified by Service

---

### Tax Credit Mechanism (Section 177)

**Economic Development Tax Credit (EDTC):**
- Tax payable during priority period = EDTC
- **Can offset:** Tax payable in priority period
- **Cannot offset:** Additional tax under Section 57 (minimum ETR)

**Carry Forward:** Unutilized EDTC carried forward 5 years post-priority period

---

### Incentive Period (Section 178)

**Duration:** 5 years from production day

**Extension:** Additional 5 years if 100% profits reinvested for expansion during initial period

---

### Requirements (Sections 179-180)

**Separate Records:** Priority vs non-priority business

**Annual Filing:**
- Income tax returns (as normal)
- Evidence of minimum qualifying criteria compliance

**Non-Compliance:** All income deemed non-priority if records inadequate

---

### Cancellation (Section 174)

**NIPC Cancels Certificate if:**
- Company applies for cancellation
- Priority business ceases
- Company liquidated/wound up
- Production not commenced 12 months after proposed date

**Minister May Suspend (3-month remedy period) if:**
- Conditions not met
- Contravention of provisions
- Estimates/proposals not fulfilled

**President May Cancel** (on Minister's recommendation)

---

### Withdrawal of Benefits (Section 181)

**Service May Withdraw/Discountenance EDTC:**
- Within 6 years of cancellation (normal cases)
- No time limit (fraud cases)

---

### Exclusivity (Section 183)

**One Incentive Only:**
- Cannot combine EDTC with similar incentives
- Old Industrial Development (Income Tax Relief) Act certificates: run to expiry
- EDTC granted before sunset: run to expiry (even if sector removed)

---

### Plantation Industry (Section 182)

**Special Rule:**
- Business deemed commenced when planting reaches commercial production
- Pre-production maintenance costs = qualifying plantation expenditure

---

## PRACTICAL EXAMPLE

### Manufacturing Company - EDTC

**Facts:**
- Incorporated 2026
- Priority sector: Pharmaceutical manufacturing
- Capital investment: N200 million (>minimum threshold)
- Production day: 1 July 2027

**Tax Treatment:**

**Years 1-5 (2027-2032):**
- Annual profit: N50 million
- Tax @ 30%: N15 million
- **EDTC earned: N15 million**
- **Tax payable: N0** (offset by EDTC)

**Year 6 (2033):**
- Priority period ends
- Normal taxation resumes
- Any unutilized EDTC from Years 1-5 can offset tax for Years 6-10

**Minimum ETR (Section 57):**
- If MNE group member or turnover >N20 billion
- 15% minimum tax still applies
- EDTC CANNOT offset this additional tax

---

## EXPORT PROCESSING ZONES (Second Schedule)

### Section 60 & Second Schedule

**Full Tax Exemption if:**
- 100% export sales OR serve export inputs, AND
- ≤25% sales to Nigerian customs territory

**Partial Taxation:**
- >25% customs territory sales: tax on ALL customs sales

**Full Taxation from 1 January 2028:**
- All customs territory sales taxed (regardless of %)
- President may extend exemption max 10 years from commencement

**Compliance Required:**
- Registration
- Filing returns
- Tax deduction at source

**Evidence:** Export proceeds or imported raw materials/equipment

---

## KEY TAKEAWAYS

1. **EDTI replaces old pioneer status**
2. **Presidential approval required** for certificates
3. **5-year base period** (extendable to 10)
4. **Tax credit mechanism** (not exemption)
5. **Strict eligibility and monitoring**
6. **Cannot stack incentives**
7. **Minimum ETR (15%) overrides EDTC** for large companies
